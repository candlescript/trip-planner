CUISINE

KOSHARI

Short description–A dish with Rice, Lentils, Pasta, Tomato sauce and fried onions

It is a traditional Egyptian staple, mixing pasta, Egyptian fried rice, vermicelli and brown lentils, and topped with chickpeas, a garlicky tomato sauce, garlic vinegar, and crispy fried onions.

FUL-MEDAMES

Short description–Bean stew with eggs

Ful medames, or simply fūl, is a stew of cooked fava beans served with olive oil, cumin, and optionally with chopped parsley, garlic, onion, lemon juice, chili pepper and other vegetables, herbs, and spices. Ful medames is traditionally made in and served out of a large metal jug.

HAMAM-MAHSHI

Short description–Stuffed pigeon mostly stuffed with rice or wheat

Hamam Mahshi is a traditional Egyptian dish of stuffed pigeons . It's a delicacy often served for special occasions. The pigeons, typically young and plump squabs, are marinated and stuffed with a seasoned mixture of rice or freekeh (toasted green wheat), onions, chopped giblets, and a variety of spices. Common spices include cinnamon, cumin, and pepper. Nuts like pine nuts or almonds may also be added for extra richness.

FATTAH

Short description–Rice and Lamb

Spiced boiled lamb/beef served over a bed of rice and toasted pita bread then served with garlicky warm tomato sauce.

HAWAWSHI

Short description–Meat Pie

Hawawshi is a traditional Egyptian dish. It is a pita stuffed with minced meat and spiced with onions, pepper, parsley, and occasionally chilies.

MACARONI BILL BESHAMEL

Short description–Pasta cooked with Ground beef and cheese

Macaroni Bechamel is a traditional Egyptian baked pasta dish known for its rich and comforting flavors. Here's a breakdown of its components:
Pasta: Typically macaroni is used, but penne or rigatoni are also popular choices.
Bechamel Sauce: This creamy white sauce is made with butter, flour, and milk, and is originated in France.
Spiced Meat Sauce: This savory layer often includes ground meat and tomatoes, seasoned with various spices.
Cheese (Optional): Some recipes add a layer of cheese for extra richness and a satisfying gratinated top.
The assembled dish is baked until golden brown and bubbly, creating a delicious and satisfying meal.

SAYADIEH

Short description–White fish with yellow rice

Sayadieh, a delightful fish and rice dish from Lebanon, boasts a symphony of flavors. Flaky white fish, traditionally cooked whole for its flavorful stock, sits atop fluffy rice seasoned with a unique spice blend typically including spices like cumin, coriander, cinnamon and caraway. Caramelized onions add sweetness and depth, while toasted nuts like almonds or pine nuts provide a delightful textural contrast. This dish may also include a decadent layer of cheese for extra richness in some variations.

OMM ALI

Short description–Baked pudding with nuts

Omm Ali is a baked pudding made using bread or flaked pastry in a tagine (ceramic bowl), with milk, sugar, nuts, and cream poured on top before it is placed under the grill which creates its signature golden color.

EGYPTIAN CHICKEN SOFRITO

Short description–Braised chicken with potatoes and onions

Egyptian Chicken Sofrito is a comforting dish featuring braised chicken, potatoes, and onions. Skinless chicken pieces, typically dark meat, are browned alongside sliced onions. Cubed or wedged potatoes are often fried for a crispy exterior before joining the pot. A simple braising liquid made with oil, lemon juice, and sometimes water or broth simmers everything together, infused with warm spices like turmeric and cardamom. Variations might include additional vegetables or a touch of spice, and vegetarian options often replace chicken with chickpeas or lentils. This flavorful one-pot meal is perfect for a satisfying and easy dinner.

KUPPOOS AND HUMMUS

Short description–Kuppoos refers to a type of flatbread commonly found in Middle Eastern cuisine. Hummus is a savory and creamy dip

Kuppoos are typically made from simple ingredients like flour, water, yeast, and salt. The dough is rolled out flat and baked in a hot oven or on a griddle until slightly puffed and golden brown. Hummus is typically prepared by blending or mashing cooked chickpeas with tahini (sesame seed paste), olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and spices like cumin until it reaches a smooth and creamy consistency.

ROZ LE LABAN

Short description–Rice pudding with crushed pistachios

Roz Le Laban literally translates to Rice with yoghurt. The Egyptian version of the Roz Le Laban is infused with different spices and topped with crushed pistachios. Don't miss eating rice pudding in an oriental restaurant in Egypt, such as Khan-El-Khalili, where an array of toppings are available to make your palette tingle, from traditional nuts, to honey, cream, ice-cream, and fresh fruits.

QATAYEF

Short description–Sweet or savory mini pancakes

Qatayef is like a sweet version of Spanish empanadas. Yeast, simple flour, and water batter is used to cook small, one sided, mini pancake circles. These are full of aerated holes at the top and sealed from the bottom as the dough touches the pan, just like an unflipped pancake. The cooked mini pancakes are usually stuffed with a nut, raisin, and sugar mixture for the sweet versions, and cheese or mincemeat mixtures for savory versions

BASBOOSA

Short description–Sweet syrup soaked semolina cake

The semolina flour-based Basboosa is also one of the cheapest desserts available. Eaten plain, with nuts, clotted cream, or both, Basboosa will be difficult to forget.

KUNAFA

Short description–Fried shredded dough with cheese filling

The traditional Egyptian version of Kunafa can be filled with a nut mixture with sugar, cream, vanilla puddings or mangoes. Imported or modernized versions include mild-cheese fillings such as Ricotta, custard, or single layered Konafa with nutella or even half a red velvet cake on top.
