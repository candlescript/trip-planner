CUISINE

KOSHARI

Short description–A dish with Rice, Lentils, Pasta, Tomato sauce and fried onions

It is a traditional Egyptian staple, mixing pasta, Egyptian fried rice, vermicelli and brown lentils, and topped with chickpeas, a garlicky tomato sauce, garlic vinegar, and crispy fried onions.

FUL-MEDAMES

Short description–Bean stew with eggs

Ful medames, or simply fūl, is a stew of cooked fava beans served with olive oil, cumin, and optionally with chopped parsley, garlic, onion, lemon juice, chili pepper and other vegetables, herbs, and spices. Ful medames is traditionally made in and served out of a large metal jug.

HAMAM-MAHSHI

Short description–Stuffed pigeon mostly stuffed with rice or wheat

Hamam Mahshi is a traditional Egyptian dish of stuffed pigeons . It's a delicacy often served for special occasions. The pigeons, typically young and plump squabs, are marinated and stuffed with a seasoned mixture of rice or freekeh (toasted green wheat), onions, chopped giblets, and a variety of spices. Common spices include cinnamon, cumin, and pepper. Nuts like pine nuts or almonds may also be added for extra richness.

FATTAH

Short description–Rice and Lamb

Spiced boiled lamb/beef served over a bed of rice and toasted pita bread then served with garlicky warm tomato sauce.

HAWAWSHI

Short description–Meat Pie

Hawawshi is a traditional Egyptian dish. It is a pita stuffed with minced meat and spiced with onions, pepper, parsley, and occasionally chilies.

MACARONI BILL BESHAMEL

Short description–Pasta cooked with Ground beef and cheese

Macaroni Bechamel is a traditional Egyptian baked pasta dish known for its rich and comforting flavors. Here's a breakdown of its components:
Pasta: Typically macaroni is used, but penne or rigatoni are also popular choices.
Bechamel Sauce: This creamy white sauce is made with butter, flour, and milk, and is originated in France.
Spiced Meat Sauce: This savory layer often includes ground meat and tomatoes, seasoned with various spices.
Cheese (Optional): Some recipes add a layer of cheese for extra richness and a satisfying gratinated top.
The assembled dish is baked until golden brown and bubbly, creating a delicious and satisfying meal.

SAYADIEH

Short description–White fish with yellow rice

Sayadieh, a delightful fish and rice dish from Lebanon, boasts a symphony of flavors. Flaky white fish, traditionally cooked whole for its flavorful stock, sits atop fluffy rice seasoned with a unique spice blend typically including spices like cumin, coriander, cinnamon and caraway. Caramelized onions add sweetness and depth, while toasted nuts like almonds or pine nuts provide a delightful textural contrast. This dish may also include a decadent layer of cheese for extra richness in some variations.
